Blackjack Basic Strategy

Basic strategy is the mathematically optimal way to play blackjack for the best long-term results. This tool provides recommendations based on standard blackjack rules (dealer stands on soft 17, double after split allowed, etc.).

Key Concepts:

  • Hard Total: A hand without an Ace, or with an Ace counted as 1
  • Soft Total: A hand with an Ace counted as 11
  • Pair: Two cards of the same rank
  • Blackjack: Ace + 10-value card (pays 3:2 typically)

Action Meanings:

  • Hit (Add): Take another card
  • Stand (Cancel): Keep your current hand
  • Double Down: Double your bet and take exactly one more card
  • Split: Split a pair into two separate hands (requires additional bet)
  • Bust: Hand total exceeds 21 (automatic loss)

General Strategy Tips:

  • Always split Aces and 8s
  • Never split 10-value cards (10, J, Q, K)
  • Double down on 11 unless dealer shows an Ace
  • Stand on hard 17 or higher
  • Hit soft 17 (Ace-6) against dealer's 2 or 7+
  • Stand on soft 18 (Ace-7) against dealer 2, 7, or 8
  • Double soft 13-18 against appropriate dealer upcards

Rule Variations:

This tool assumes standard rules where the dealer stands on soft 17. If playing with different rules (like dealer hits soft 17), some strategy decisions may vary slightly.
